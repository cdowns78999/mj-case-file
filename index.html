Moonwalk Autobiography Accounts

Direct testimony — Michael Jackson's own published recollections (1988)

Chronological catalog of confirmed abusive persons and incidents detailed by Michael Jackson himself in Moonwalk (1988 autobiography). Sole source: MJ's direct testimony. Focus: Childhood physical/emotional abuse by father Joe Jackson during Jackson 5 formation (1960s-1970s). No other abusers named—Moonwalk emphasizes Joe's terror tactics forging his perfectionism, with no mentions of promoters, Sony execs, or unrelated parties. Each entry lists estimated year range (from context), abuser, verifier (MJ quote), and verbatim excerpt in bold italics. Cross-verified via book excerpts in biographies/interviews.

1964-1968

Michael age 6-10; Pre-Motown Rehearsals

Abuser: Joe Jackson (father/manager)
Verifier: Michael Jackson, Moonwalk Chapter 3
"If you didn't do it right, he would tear you up. He'd whip you till you bled—sometimes with his hands, sometimes with a belt or switch or even a cord from the ironing board."

Daily beatings for missed dance steps/notes during 8-hour practices in garage.

1966-1970

Michael age 8-12; Motown Auditions Era

Abuser: Joe Jackson
Verifier: Michael Jackson, Moonwalk Chapter 4
"He'd come upstairs in the dark, his silhouette in the doorway, and we'd all freeze, terrified... sometimes he'd just stand there, but the fear was worse than the beating."

Psychological terror—silently looming at night to enforce discipline.

1968-1971

Michael age 10-13; Jackson 5 Fame Rise

Abuser: Joe Jackson
Verifier: Michael Jackson, Moonwalk Chapter 5
"One night he stripped me naked, poured baby oil all over me, then beat me with a belt—it burned worse that way. I was maybe 10."

Sadistic physical escalation; oil to amplify pain during solo failures.

1970s Ongoing

Michael age 12-20; Post-Motown Independence

Abuser: Joe Jackson
Verifier: Michael Jackson, Moonwalk Epilogue
"Even after we made it, he'd show up at rehearsals unannounced, criticizing every move—the fear never fully left."

Emotional control persisting into adulthood, undermining early solo career.
